Historical Context

El Filibusterismo captures the dire situations within the Philippines stemming from Spanish torment. This was the reality while Rizal was in exile in Europe. Noli Me Tangere focuses on extending requests for changes, while El Filibusterismo presents a more extreme viewpoint, which aligns with the now widespread revolutionary Culture of the region.

Comparison with Noli Me Tangere

With fears firmly stationed at the forefront, Noli Me Tangere argues for change with an optimistic tone, however El filibusterismo tells a much wider and deeper story. Simoun in the new incarnation of Ibarra, where Ibarra is now molded into abandoning all attempts at asking change in a peaceful form.

Motivation and Influence

Rizal intended to reveal the abuse of power by the Spanish authorities and Clergy, while simultaneously hoping to inspire Filipinos to claim their rights, even if it meant going to the extent of revolution. His work influenced the Philippine Revolution (1896) and remains a crucial text in Philippine history.

Impact and Legacy

  • One of the reasons why El Filibusterismo is a part of the Philippine curriculum is to ensure that Rizal's lessons continue to impact the youth.
  • It significantly helped shape the sentiments of the Indios and the interpreter of Spain.
  • Social issues, abuse of governmental power, and nationalistic sentiments are all still relevant to the themes of the novel.

Key Characters

Simoun (Crisóstomo Ibarra)

A wealthy and mysterious jeweler who is actually Ibarra in disguise. He seeks vengeance against the Spanish authorities and plans a revolution.

Basilio

A medical student whose mother, Sisa, suffered greatly in Noli Me Tangere. He represents the youth's struggle for education and justice.

Isagani

A wealthy and mysterious jeweler who is actually Ibarra in disguise. He seeks vengeance against the Spanish authorities and plans a revolution.

Kabesang Tale

A former farmer turned bandit due to the injustices of Spanish landlords and friars. His story represents the oppression of Filipino peasants.
